After taking Web Programming in college at Georgia State University, I fell in love with Front-End Web Development. This particular course covered HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP. I was able to get a good grasp on the foundational aspects of Web Programming and was able to further utilize and enhance my skills in my Software Engineering course. I specifically worked on Front-End projects such as the popular Game of Life, which allowed me to understand the fundamentals of JavaScript and how it is incorporated with HTML and CSS. Overall, Front-End work is considered a strong suit of mine, and I would love to continue to learn more about it.

Back-End Development

My bachelor's degree at Georgia State University allowed me to gain most of my knowledge on Back-End development. A substantial amount of my learning was attained by a Meta engineer whom taught my Software Engineering course at my University and encouraged me to work on my most vital extracurricular work I have today. This allowed me to grasp my key back-end engineering abilities with Verison Control, RESTful APIs, Databases, Python, and various Frameworks. I then expanded on my back-end knowledge with team work by creating a website named "FoodFight" which allows for users to create groups and decide which restaurants to eat at based on their ratings. This made use of Yelp's Rest API, and used a lot of back-end work using Flask, Flask Login, PostgreSQL, and Python.
